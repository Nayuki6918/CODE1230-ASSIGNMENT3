From 2011 to 2016 increasing of educatees

According to NSW Education Centre, Since 2011, the NSW Government has embarked on a series of ambitious reforms aimed to improve education outcomes in NSW. The reforms focus on improving learning level for rural and remote students, taking new approaches to improve the education and social aspirations of Aboriginal children and teenagers(NSW Education Centre.2016). From the map of the differences of educatees, it can represent the result and improvement of these policies. In the west and northwest area, the increasing data is less than other sites, it may because of the low population density in these cities. On the east coast, the increasing of educatees was above than average, especially in Newcastle, Sydney and Wollongong.





Conclusion

● The increasing number of educatees and population size higher than average in Sydney，Newcastle and Wollongong.
● The quality of education in the eastern coast higher than the west and northwest area.
● Women in major cities are more likely to hold qualifications of college degrees than women in regional or remote locations.
